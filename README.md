# Comparative Financial Statement & Risk-Return Analysis: Safe Mix Concrete vs. Thatta Cement

## 📌 Project Overview
This repository contains a comprehensive financial statement evaluation and risk-return analysis comparing **Safe Mix Concrete Limited (SMC)** and **Thatta Cement Company Limited**, covering the fiscal years 2023 through 2025 alongside stock market returns up to May 2026. 

The study utilizes core corporate finance methodologies—including liquidity, solvency, activity, and profitability ratio trends, vertical and horizontal (index) financial statement analysis, and stock return volatility profiling—to evaluate operational performance, financial leverage risks, and market investment viability.

---

## 📁 Repository Structure
The project is organized into the following directories for ease of academic review:
* `Data_and_Models/`: Contains the master financial engineering file (`Final year project BF.xlsx`). This workbook includes four primary analytical layers:
    * **Sheet 1:** Raw Income Statement and Balance Sheet data (2023–2025).
    * **Sheet 2:** Derived financial metrics (Liquidity, Solvency, Coverage, and Activity Ratios).
    * **Sheet 3:** Vertical analysis (common-size percentages) and Horizontal index analysis.
    * **Sheet 4:** Statistical stock return deviations, historical pricing, and asset mean variances.
* `Reports/`: Contains the comprehensive academic manuscript detailing the background context, analytical literature, and final interpretations in both editable (`.docx`) and submission-ready (`.pdf`) formats.

## 📊 Summary of Key Methodologies & Findings

### 1. Trend & Ratio Analysis
* **Liquidity:** Safe Mix Concrete maintained stable short-term liquidity profiles with a Current Ratio hovering around 1.38 to 1.42 (2024–2025), driven by robust cash and bank balances. Thatta Cement demonstrated highly conservative liquid structuring with a Current Ratio of 2.41 in 2025.
* **Leverage & Capital Structure:** SMC utilizes a higher leverage structure, carrying a Debt-to-Equity ratio of 1.24 in 2025, which introduces notable fixed interest obligations. Conversely, Thatta Cement relies heavily on equity financing, showing zero long-term debt on its capital structure for the same period.
* **Coverage:** SMC's Times Interest Earned (TIE) ratio bounced back up to 5.07 in 2025, showing their operating profit is healthy enough to cover interest payments comfortably. However, Thatta Cement leads with an exceptional TIE of 39.12 due to its debt-free capital model.

### 2. Common-Size (Vertical) & Index (Horizontal) Insights
* **Cost Management:** Vertical common-size analysis reveals that SMC experienced a squeeze on profit margins due to rising manufacturing costs, with its Cost of Sales scaling from 77.5% of net revenues in 2023 to 84.5% in 2025.
* **Growth Trajectory:** Horizontal index analysis shows that while SMC successfully expanded its top-line revenue index to 1.15 by 2025 (a 15.3% growth over the 2023 baseline), escalating structural production costs outpaced revenues, shrinking net margins.

### 3. Investment Risk-Return Performance
* **Historical Market Returns:** Over the analyzed trading periods ending in May 2026, both securities encountered systemic market pressures on the local stock exchange, yielding negative average daily statistical returns (SMC: -0.07%, Thatta Cement: -0.21%).
* **Volatility Analysis:** Square deviation calculations show that market price fluctuations remain closely matched between the two firms, indicating shared sector risks linked to public infrastructure developments and macro-economic factors.
